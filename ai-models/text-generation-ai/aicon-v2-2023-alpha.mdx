Our AiCon Alpha models are built on the innovative foundation of our AiCon v3 models, designed to harness the power of the internet for real-time knowledge and insights. This enables AiCon Alpha to provide answers that are not only accurate but also reflect the latest information available online. Here's a closer look at how AiCon Alpha operates:

- **Foundation on AiCon v3 Models**: AiCon Alpha extends the capabilities of our AiCon v3 models, leveraging their advanced architecture to deliver enhanced performance and understanding.

- **In-house Search Technology**: We've developed a proprietary search, indexing, and crawling infrastructure that allows AiCon Alpha to access the most relevant and current information. Our search index is expansive, regularly updated, and employs advanced ranking algorithms to highlight high-quality, authentic content. This ensures that AiCon Alpha can offer responses enriched with the latest data, thanks to website excerpts or "snippets" drawn directly from our search results.

- **Fine-tuning for Real-time Relevance**: The AiCon Alpha models undergo rigorous fine-tuning, utilizing carefully selected, high-quality training sets prepared by our in-house data specialists. This process is aimed at optimizing the models' performance across various metrics, including accuracy, relevance, and timeliness. Regular updates and fine-tuning sessions ensure that AiCon Alpha remains at the forefront of AI-driven, internet-aware response generation.

By integrating these elements, AiCon Alpha stands out as a dynamic, internet-aware model capable of addressing queries with up-to-the-minute accuracy, such as recent event outcomes or the latest developments in a given field. This positions AiCon Alpha as a cutting-edge tool for accessing and leveraging internet-based knowledge.

<Tip>However, the Alpha Nightly channel is our most recent API Release, and is currently in active development. This channel is not recommended for production use, and is subject to change at any time.</Tip>

you are able to send the Headers and the Request Body to the API Endpoint. It's that easy! Just
send a POST Request to the API Endpoint with the Headers and the Request Body, and you are good to go!
